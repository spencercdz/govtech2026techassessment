# Marketplace Insights for University Context

## 1) Common User Pain Points
- Listing friction: students are unsure about pricing, condition descriptions, and photo quality.
- Trust uncertainty: buyers struggle to assess whether sellers are legitimate and responsive.
- Coordination issues: no-shows, late arrivals, and vague pickup instructions are frequent.
- Disputes: mismatched item condition and payment confusion create support load.

## 2) Safety and Trust Challenges (Campus-Specific)
- Students may over-trust peers due to shared school affiliation, which scammers can exploit.
- Dorm and campus movement constraints can create unsafe or inconvenient meetup behavior.
- Harassment risks are elevated when real identities are known within the same campus community.
- Prohibited-item attempts (medication, lab chemicals, fake IDs, stolen electronics) require strict moderation.

## 3) Seasonal Patterns
- Semester start: high demand for textbooks, calculators, dorm essentials, bikes.
- Mid-semester: lower transaction volume; more niche and course-specific item exchanges.
- Finals period: increased scheduling conflicts and delayed pickups.
- Move-out and graduation windows: large spikes in furniture/appliance listings and urgency-driven pricing.

## 4) Integration Needs with University Systems
- Institutional email verification to prevent outsider abuse.
- Optional integration with student directory status (active student/alumni) for trust signals.
- Campus safety channel integration for high-risk incidents.
- SSO and identity lifecycle sync to disable access when accounts are no longer eligible.

## 5) Implications for Chatbot Requirements
- Must support quick role-based flows (new user, buyer, seller).
- Must apply strict safety guardrails with clear escalation pathways.
- Must adapt recommendations to campus calendar peaks.
- Must provide concise, actionable checklists for transactions and disputes.
